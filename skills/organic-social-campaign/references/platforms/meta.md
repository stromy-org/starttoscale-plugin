# Meta (Facebook & Instagram) Organic — B2B Reference

## Strategic Context for B2B

Meta platforms are primarily consumer-oriented. For B2B corporate services, organic Meta presence serves specific purposes:

- **Retargeting seed**: Build pixel audiences from organic visitors for paid campaigns
- **Brand credibility**: Prospects who Google you will check your social presence — an active page signals legitimacy
- **Recruitment**: Employer brand content performs well on Meta (team culture, office, events)
- **Event promotion**: Facebook Events still drives registrations, especially for local/regional events
- **Client-facing proof**: Some B2B clients (especially government and enterprise) check vendor social media as part of due diligence

**When to skip organic Meta for B2B**: If resources are limited, invest in LinkedIn first. A dormant Meta page is worse than no page — it signals neglect. Only maintain Meta if you can sustain 2-3 posts per week minimum.

## Business Page Setup

### Facebook Page Optimization

- **Profile photo**: 170x170px, use logo (consistent with LinkedIn)
- **Cover photo**: 820x312px (desktop), keep key elements in center 640px for mobile crop
- **About section**: Concise, keyword-rich. Include location, hours, contact info
- **Action button**: "Contact Us", "Send Message", or "Learn More" — match your primary CTA
- **Page category**: Choose the most specific category available for your industry
- **Services tab**: List service offerings with descriptions (helps local search)
- **Milestones**: Add founding date, major achievements (builds credibility)

### Instagram Business Profile

- **Profile photo**: 320x320px, logo
- **Bio**: 150 chars max. State what you do + who you serve + CTA. Use line breaks for readability
- **Link in bio**: Use a link-in-bio tool (Linktree or equivalent) if you need multiple destinations
- **Contact buttons**: Enable email, phone, and address
- **Category**: Match Facebook page category
- **Highlights**: Create 3-5 themed Story Highlights (Services, Team, Case Studies, Events, About)

## Content Formats

### Facebook

| Format | Best For (B2B) |
|--------|----------------|
| **Text + image** | Industry news, team highlights, event promotion |
| **Video** | Explainers, event recaps, testimonials (60-90s optimal) |
| **Link post** | Blog/article promotion (note: reduced organic reach) |
| **Carousel** | Multi-image storytelling, process walkthroughs |
| **Live** | Webinar simulcasts, Q&A sessions |
| **Event** | Webinar promotion, conference attendance, meetups |
| **Reel** | Short-form video (15-90s), behind-the-scenes |

### Instagram

| Format | Best For (B2B) |
|--------|----------------|
| **Feed post (image)** | Branded graphics, data visualizations, quotes |
| **Carousel** | Frameworks, checklists, case study slides (up to 20 slides) |
| **Reel** | Short video content, trending formats adapted to B2B |
| **Stories** | Day-of events, polls, quick updates (24hr, saveable to Highlights) |
| **Live** | Panels, interviews, behind-the-scenes |

### B2B Format Priorities

For resource-constrained B2B teams, prioritize in this order:
1. **Instagram carousels**: Highest organic reach on Instagram, repurposable from LinkedIn carousels
2. **Reels**: Algorithm-favored on both platforms, 15-30 seconds is sufficient
3. **Facebook events**: Genuine utility for driving registrations
4. **Feed posts with images**: Baseline content to keep pages active

## Algorithm Signals

### Facebook

- **Meaningful interactions**: Comments and shares weighted heavily over reactions
- **Content from friends vs. pages**: Personal connections prioritized — employee shares reach more people than page posts
- **Video completion rate**: How much of a video people watch
- **Recency**: Facebook refreshes feed more frequently than LinkedIn
- **Content type preference**: The algorithm learns what formats each user engages with and shows more of the same

### Instagram

- **Interest**: Predicted likelihood the user will engage based on past behavior
- **Timeliness**: Newer posts prioritized
- **Relationship**: Frequency of interaction between the user and the account
- **Saves and shares**: Weighted more heavily than likes in 2024+
- **Reels algorithm**: Separate from feed — optimized for entertainment value and watch-through rate

### What Suppresses Reach on Meta

- Link posts without native content (just a URL with minimal text)
- Engagement bait ("Like if you agree, share if you disagree")
- Posting identical content across Facebook and Instagram simultaneously
- Inactive periods followed by posting bursts
- Low-quality images or watermarked content (especially from other platforms)

## Posting Best Practices

### Cadence

- **Facebook**: 3-5 posts per week. More than once per day actively hurts reach per post
- **Instagram feed**: 3-5 posts per week
- **Instagram Stories**: 3-7 per week (can do multiple per day without penalty)
- **Reels**: 2-4 per week for growth phase, 1-2 per week for maintenance

### Timing (B2B)

- **Facebook**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9:00-11:00 AM
- **Instagram**: Tuesday-Friday, 10:00 AM-1:00 PM
- Use Meta Business Suite's "optimal time" suggestions once you have 30+ days of data

### Cross-Posting Strategy

- **Do**: Adapt content between platforms (reformat, adjust caption length, change hashtag strategy)
- **Do not**: Auto-cross-post identical content. Instagram and Facebook audiences expect different tones
- **LinkedIn to Instagram**: Carousel content translates well — redesign for square/portrait format
- **LinkedIn to Facebook**: Shorten thought leadership posts, add more visual elements

## Analytics (Meta Business Suite)

### Available Metrics

- **Reach**: Unique accounts that saw your content
- **Impressions**: Total views (includes repeat views)
- **Engagement**: Reactions, comments, shares, saves, clicks
- **Follower demographics**: Age, gender, location, active times
- **Content performance**: Per-post metrics with format comparison
- **Website traffic**: Referral clicks (pair with UTM tracking for accuracy)

### Key Metrics for B2B

- **Engagement rate**: (Engagements / Reach) x 100
- **Save rate** (Instagram): Saves / Reach — indicates content utility
- **Video retention**: Average watch time and drop-off points
- **Profile visits**: Leading indicator of brand curiosity
- **Website clicks**: The metric that actually ties to business outcomes

## B2B Content Strategy on Meta

### What Works

- **Employer brand content**: Team photos, culture moments, hiring announcements — this is where Meta outperforms LinkedIn for some companies
- **Visual case studies**: Before/after, data graphics, project photos
- **Event content**: Pre-event teasers, live coverage, post-event recaps
- **Short-form video**: Reels of 15-30 seconds with a single insight or tip
- **Behind-the-scenes**: Office, team retreats, project work in progress

### What Does Not Work

- Long text-heavy thought leadership (that belongs on LinkedIn)
- Constant self-promotion without value
- Purely corporate announcements with no visual hook
- Content that requires deep industry context to understand
- Repurposed LinkedIn posts without format adaptation

### Meta as a Retargeting Seed

Even with modest organic reach, Meta organic content serves paid strategy:

1. Organic posts build a warm audience (page visitors, video viewers, post engagers)
2. These audiences become custom audiences for paid campaigns
3. Lookalike audiences expand from engaged organic followers
4. Cost per acquisition drops because the seed audience is pre-qualified

This makes maintaining even a modest organic presence worthwhile if paid Meta campaigns are in the plan.

## When to Invest vs. When to Deprioritize

### Invest in Meta Organic When

- You plan to run paid Meta campaigns (organic feeds the pixel and builds audiences)
- Your sector includes stakeholders who are active on Instagram (design, creative, younger demographics)
- Employer branding is a strategic priority
- You host events that benefit from Facebook Events promotion
- Your clients expect to find you on Meta as a credibility signal

### Deprioritize When

- Resources only support one platform well (choose LinkedIn for B2B)
- Your audience is exclusively C-suite or senior government (low Meta engagement)
- You have no paid Meta campaigns planned
- Your content is highly technical or niche (better served by LinkedIn or YouTube)
