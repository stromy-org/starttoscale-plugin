# YouTube Organic — B2B Reference

## Strategic Context for B2B

YouTube is the second-largest search engine. For B2B corporate services, it serves distinct purposes:

- **Evergreen search traffic**: YouTube videos rank in Google search results — a well-optimized video can drive leads for years
- **Credibility and depth**: Long-form video demonstrates expertise in ways short-form content cannot
- **Webinar and event archive**: Recordings become searchable, discoverable assets
- **Shorts for discovery**: YouTube Shorts feed new audiences into your channel ecosystem
- **Sales enablement**: "Watch our 3-minute explainer" is a powerful mid-funnel asset

**Key difference from other platforms**: YouTube is a search and recommendation engine, not a social feed. Content lifespan is months to years, not hours to days. This changes the entire content strategy — optimize for discovery, not recency.

## Channel Setup

### Essentials

- **Channel name**: Company name (consistent with other platforms)
- **Handle**: `@companyname` — claim early, keep short
- **Profile photo**: 800x800px, logo
- **Banner**: 2560x1440px, safe area 1546x423px (center). Include tagline, posting schedule, or CTA
- **Channel description**: First 100 characters appear in search. Lead with what the channel offers viewers, not company history. Include relevant keywords
- **Channel trailer**: 30-60 second video for non-subscribers explaining what the channel covers and why to subscribe
- **Featured video**: For returning subscribers, pin your latest or most important video
- **Links**: Website, LinkedIn, other social profiles (appears on channel page)
- **Contact email**: Enable for business inquiries

### Channel Organization

- **Playlists**: Group videos by topic, series, or audience segment. Playlists rank in search independently
- **Sections**: Organize channel page with sections (Popular uploads, Playlists, Recent uploads)
- **Featured channels**: Link to partner or related channels (builds ecosystem)
- **Community tab**: Available after meeting subscriber threshold — use for polls, updates, engagement

### Branding

- **Watermark**: Upload a subscribe button watermark (appears on all videos)
- **End screen template**: Design a consistent end screen layout for CTAs and video suggestions
- **Thumbnail style**: Establish a consistent visual template — recognizable thumbnails drive click-through rate more than any other factor

## Content Formats

| Format | Specs | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Long-form video** | 8-20min optimal for B2B, up to 12hrs | Deep explainers, presentations, interviews |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Up to 60s, vertical (9:16) | Quick tips, hooks to long-form, trending formats |
| **Live stream** | Unlimited duration | Webinars, panels, Q&A sessions |
| **Premieres** | Scheduled video with live chat | Event-style launches for important content |
| **Community posts** | Text, images, polls, GIFs | Audience engagement between videos |
| **Podcast** | Audio-first with optional video | Long-form discussions (YouTube is now a top podcast platform) |

### Format Strategy for B2B

- **Long-form (8-15 min)**: The core format. Well-structured explainers and case studies perform best
- **Shorts**: Use as a discovery tool — hook viewers with a 30-second insight, direct them to the full video
- **Live/Premiere**: Reserve for events with promotional lead-up. Raw live streams without promotion underperform
- **Podcast format**: Growing rapidly. Upload video podcast episodes as long-form content — YouTube is the second most popular podcast platform

## SEO for B2B YouTube

YouTube search optimization is the highest-leverage activity for B2B channels. Unlike social platforms, YouTube content is discovered through search for months or years after publication.

### Title Optimization

- Front-load keywords ("Stakeholder Engagement Strategy: 5 Steps That Actually Work" not "5 Steps for Better Stakeholder Engagement Strategy")
- 60 characters max (truncates in search results)
- Include the specific problem the video solves
- Avoid clickbait that mismatches content (damages watch time metrics)

### Description

- First 2-3 lines appear in search results — make them count
- Include primary keyword in the first sentence
- Write 200-500 words of genuine description (YouTube indexes this for search)
- Include timestamps/chapters (improves UX and creates additional search entry points)
- Add links: website, related videos, social profiles
- Include a CTA (subscribe, download related resource, contact)

### Tags

- Include 5-10 relevant tags
- Primary keyword as first tag
- Mix of broad ("B2B marketing") and specific ("stakeholder engagement framework consulting")
- Include common misspellings of key terms
- Tags matter less than title and description but still contribute to discovery

### Chapters (Timestamps)

- Add timestamps in the description starting with `0:00`
- YouTube creates clickable chapters from these timestamps
- Each chapter becomes a potential search result entry point
- Optimal chapter length: 2-5 minutes each

### Thumbnails

- 1280x720px, 2MB max
- High contrast, readable text at small sizes
- Human face with expression outperforms text-only thumbnails (even for B2B)
- Consistent style across videos for brand recognition
- A/B test thumbnails (YouTube Studio allows this)
- Do NOT just use a frame from the video — design a custom thumbnail every time

## B2B Video Content Strategy

### Content Categories

**Thought leadership** (40% of content)
- Industry analysis and trend breakdowns
- Framework explanations with examples
- "State of [industry topic]" recurring series
- Point-of-view pieces on sector developments

**Educational/How-to** (30% of content)
- Step-by-step methodology walkthroughs
- Tool and process demonstrations
- "How we do X at [company]" behind-the-methodology content
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them

**Case studies and proof points** (15% of content)
- Client outcome stories (anonymized or with permission)
- Before/after walkthroughs
- Project deep-dives showing methodology in action

**Interviews and conversations** (15% of content)
- Industry expert interviews
- Client conversations
- Team member spotlights
- Webinar recordings (edited to remove dead time)

### Production Tiers

Not all videos need high production value. Match production to content type:

| Tier | Production | Content Type | Equipment |
|------|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **Quick** | Minimal editing, talking head | Tips, hot takes, Shorts | Webcam + good mic + ring light |
| **Standard** | Basic editing, graphics, chapters | Explainers, how-tos | Camera + mic + basic editing |
| **Polished** | Full editing, motion graphics, B-roll | Flagship content, case studies | Professional setup or contracted |

Start with Quick tier to build consistency. Upgrade production as the channel grows.

### Video Structure for B2B

1. **Hook** (0-15 seconds): State the problem or promise. No intros, no logos, no "hey guys"
2. **Context** (15-60 seconds): Why this matters to the viewer
3. **Body** (bulk): Deliver the value. Use chapters, visual aids, and examples
4. **Recap** (30 seconds): Summarize key takeaways
5. **CTA** (15 seconds): Subscribe, watch next video, download resource
6. **End screen** (20 seconds): Cards linking to related videos and subscribe button

## Analytics

### Key Metrics for B2B

- **Watch time**: Total hours watched — YouTube's primary ranking signal
- **Average view duration**: How much of each video viewers watch. Above 50% is good for B2B
- **Click-through rate (CTR)**: Impressions to views. 4-10% is typical, above 8% is strong
- **Traffic sources**: Search vs. suggested vs. browse vs. external — tells you how people find you
- **Subscriber conversion**: Views to subscribers — indicates channel value perception
- **Audience retention graph**: Shows exactly where viewers drop off — use to improve future videos

### Metrics That Do Not Matter Much for B2B

- View count (a video with 500 views from your exact target audience is worth more than 50,000 random views)
- Like/dislike ratio (informational, not actionable)
- Comment count (unless engagement is a strategic goal)

## When YouTube Makes Sense for B2B

### Invest When

- Your expertise lends itself to visual explanation (frameworks, processes, data)
- You can commit to consistent publishing (minimum 2 videos per month)
- Search-driven discovery matters (people Google problems you solve)
- You have subject matter experts willing to appear on camera
- Long content lifespan is valuable (evergreen topics, not news-reactive)
- You plan to repurpose video across other channels (Shorts to social, audio to podcast)

### Deprioritize When

- You cannot sustain consistent publishing (a dead channel hurts credibility)
- Your content is highly confidential or sensitive (video is hard to retract)
- Your topics are too niche for search volume (check YouTube search autocomplete)
- No one is willing to be on camera and you lack budget for animation/graphics
- Your sales cycle does not benefit from video content (rare in B2B, but possible)
