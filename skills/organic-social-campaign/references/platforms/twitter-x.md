# X (Twitter) Organic — B2B Reference

## Strategic Context for B2B

X occupies a specific niche in B2B social strategy:

- **Real-time industry discourse**: Policy, regulation, breaking news, and industry events still concentrate on X
- **Journalist and media relations**: Reporters and analysts monitor X — useful for PR amplification
- **Thought leader access**: Direct engagement with industry leaders is easier on X than any other platform
- **Conference and event amplification**: Live-tweeting and event hashtags remain effective
- **Tech and policy sectors**: These verticals have disproportionately active X communities

**Limitations**: Organic reach for brand accounts is low. Individual voices (executives, team members) consistently outperform brand accounts. The platform's audience has shifted since 2022 — validate that your specific audience is still active before investing heavily.

## Profile Optimization

### Brand Account

- **Profile photo**: 400x400px, logo
- **Header image**: 1500x500px, current campaign or brand statement
- **Bio**: 160 chars. State what the company does + sector focus. Include a relevant hashtag if your industry has one
- **Location**: Include if relevant to your market
- **Website link**: UTM-tagged link to a relevant landing page (not just homepage)
- **Pinned post**: Pin your strongest recent post — a thread, a key insight, or a campaign anchor
- **Professional account**: Enable if available in your region for category labels and enhanced analytics

### Executive Accounts (Higher Priority)

For B2B, executive personal accounts often matter more than the brand account:

- **Bio**: Role + company + 1-2 areas of expertise + optional personal note
- **Consistent voice**: Establish whether the exec posts themselves or has support (audiences detect inauthenticity quickly)
- **Network building**: Follow and engage with industry peers, journalists, and policy makers before posting extensively

## Content Formats

| Format | Specs | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Text post** | 280 chars (free) / 25,000 chars (Premium) | Quick takes, commentary, engagement |
| **Image post** | Up to 4 images, 1600x900px optimal | Data charts, infographics, event photos |
| **Video** | 2:20 max (free) / 60min (Premium), 512MB | Short explainers, event clips |
| **Thread** | Series of connected posts | Long-form thought leadership, case studies |
| **Poll** | 4 options, 5min-7day duration | Audience research, engagement |
| **Spaces** | Live audio rooms | Industry discussions, panels, AMAs |
| **Long-form articles** | Premium feature | Detailed analysis (limited discovery) |

### Format Performance for B2B

- **Threads** are the highest-value format for B2B thought leadership on X. A well-structured 5-8 post thread with a strong hook can generate significant reach
- **Quote posts with commentary** outperform plain retweets and show your perspective
- **Single-image posts** with a chart or data point drive saves and shares
- **Video** underperforms on X compared to other platforms for B2B unless it is very short (under 60 seconds)
- **Polls** drive replies but the engagement quality is low

## Thread Strategy

Threads are the B2B power format on X. Structure them deliberately:

### Thread Anatomy

1. **Hook post** (post 1): The most important. Must stand alone as compelling. Use a bold claim, surprising data point, or question. Do not start with "Thread:" or "1/"
2. **Context** (post 2): Set up the problem or background
3. **Body** (posts 3-6): One idea per post. Use numbered lists, data points, or examples
4. **Conclusion** (post 7-8): Synthesize the insight. Include a question to drive replies
5. **Self-reply CTA**: After the thread, reply with a link to related content (keeps the link out of the thread itself)

### Thread Tips

- Write the hook post last (after you know what the thread actually says)
- Each post should make sense if someone only sees that one post
- Add an image to post 1 (stops the scroll) and optionally post 3-4
- Optimal thread length for B2B: 5-8 posts. Over 12 loses readers
- Post threads in the morning (7-9 AM audience timezone) for maximum initial engagement

## Posting Best Practices

### Cadence

- **Brand account**: 1-3 posts per day (including replies and quote posts)
- **Executive accounts**: 3-5 posts per week minimum for visibility
- **Threads**: 1-2 per week maximum — they require effort and audiences tire of them if overused
- **Spaces**: Monthly or tied to events — do not host unless you have confirmed speakers and promotion

### Timing

- **B2B peak**: Weekdays 7:00-9:00 AM and 12:00-1:00 PM (audience timezone)
- **News-reactive content**: Post within 2 hours of breaking news for relevance
- **Threads**: Early morning for best pickup (7:00-8:00 AM)
- **Spaces**: Tuesday-Thursday, 12:00-1:00 PM or 4:00-5:00 PM

### Engagement Patterns

- **Reply to replies**: The algorithm rewards accounts that engage in conversation, not just broadcast
- **Quote-post with substance**: Add 2-3 sentences of original perspective when sharing others' content
- **Join trending discussions**: Monitor industry hashtags and jump into relevant threads early
- **Engage before posting**: Spend 10-15 minutes engaging with others' content before publishing your own — this primes the algorithm

## Algorithm Signals

X's algorithm (post-2023 "For You" feed) weighs:

1. **Replies and conversation threads**: Back-and-forth replies signal high-quality content
2. **Bookmarks**: Strong signal of content utility
3. **Time spent on post**: Dwell time, especially on threads
4. **Recency**: X's feed refreshes faster than LinkedIn — content lifespan is hours, not days
5. **Network graph**: Content from accounts your connections engage with
6. **Premium account boost**: Verified (paid) accounts receive distribution preference

### What Suppresses Reach

- Links in the post body (X actively suppresses external links — put links in replies)
- Posting without engaging with others (broadcast-only accounts)
- Hashtag overuse (1-2 relevant hashtags max, or none)
- Cross-posted content from other platforms (especially Instagram watermarks)
- Automated posting patterns (consistent exact-time posting from scheduling tools)

## Analytics

### Available Metrics

- **Impressions**: How many times a post appeared in feeds
- **Engagements**: Likes, replies, reposts, quote posts, bookmarks, link clicks, profile visits
- **Engagement rate**: Engagements / Impressions
- **Follower growth**: Net new followers per period
- **Top posts**: Ranked by impressions or engagements
- **Video views**: For video content, views and watch time

### Key Metrics for B2B

- **Reply rate**: Replies / Impressions — indicates conversation quality
- **Bookmark rate**: Bookmarks / Impressions — indicates content utility (high-value signal for B2B)
- **Thread completion**: Impressions on last post vs. first post in a thread
- **Profile visits**: Leading indicator of brand curiosity
- **Link clicks**: (from reply links) actual traffic driven

## B2B Content Strategy

### What Works

- **Industry commentary**: Quick, informed takes on sector news. Speed matters — first mover advantage is real on X
- **Contrarian insights**: Challenge conventional wisdom with evidence. X rewards strong opinions more than LinkedIn does
- **Data and charts**: Single-image posts with a striking data visualization
- **Thread-based case studies**: Walk through a methodology or project outcome in thread format
- **Conference live-posting**: Real-time coverage of industry events (with event hashtag)
- **Curated insights**: "3 things I noticed in [industry report]" — add value to existing content

### What Does Not Work

- Corporate announcements in formal language (sounds robotic on X)
- Long text posts without formatting or hooks
- Excessive self-promotion without industry engagement
- Posting only during business hours and ignoring off-hours conversations
- Treating X like LinkedIn (different tone, different pace, different audience expectations)

### Tone Calibration

X rewards a more direct, conversational tone than LinkedIn:

- **LinkedIn**: "We're pleased to share our latest thinking on stakeholder engagement frameworks"
- **X**: "Most stakeholder engagement is theater. Here's what actually works (thread)"

The same substance, different packaging. X audiences penalize corporate-speak more aggressively than any other platform.

## When to Invest vs. When to Deprioritize

### Invest When

- Your sector has active X communities (tech, policy, government, media, finance)
- Your executives are willing to post from personal accounts
- Real-time industry events matter to your business (conferences, policy announcements, market moves)
- Media relations is a strategic priority (journalists are reachable on X)
- You have strong opinions and are willing to share them

### Deprioritize When

- Your audience is not active on X (validate with audience research, do not assume)
- No executive is willing to be the voice (brand-only accounts underperform)
- Your content requires long-form explanation that does not compress into threads
- You cannot commit to the pace X requires (daily engagement, rapid response to news)
- Risk tolerance is low — X conversations can escalate quickly
