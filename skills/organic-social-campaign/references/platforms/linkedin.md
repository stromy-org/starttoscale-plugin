# LinkedIn Organic — B2B Reference

## Page Optimization

### Completeness Checklist

Pages with complete information get 30% more weekly views. Ensure every field is filled:

- **Logo**: 300x300px, appears in search results and follower feeds
- **Cover image**: 1128x191px, reinforce brand or current campaign
- **About section**: First 156 characters appear in search previews — lead with what the company does for clients, not internal mission statements. Include 3-5 relevant keywords naturally
- **Custom button**: Set to the highest-value CTA (usually "Visit website" or "Contact us"). Track via UTM
- **Tagline**: 120 characters max. Describe what you do, not who you are ("Strategic communications for infrastructure and government" not "Award-winning consultancy")
- **Hashtags**: Add 3 community hashtags relevant to your sector. These associate your page with topic feeds
- **Specialties**: Fill all 20 slots with service keywords clients actually search for
- **Location, industry, company size**: Affects who LinkedIn surfaces your page to

### Profile Strength Signals

- Verified company page (blue checkmark) — apply through LinkedIn admin
- Custom URL slug (`linkedin.com/company/your-name`)
- Featured content section curated (pin 2-3 high-performing posts)
- Showcase Pages for distinct business lines (only if they warrant separate audiences)

## Content Formats

| Format | Specs | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Text post** | 3,000 chars max (700-1,300 optimal) | Thought leadership, hot takes, frameworks |
| **Single image** | 1200x627px (landscape) or 1080x1350px (portrait) | Data visualizations, quotes, event promotion |
| **Carousel/Document** | PDF upload, up to 300 pages, 100MB | Frameworks, guides, checklists, case study summaries |
| **Native video** | 3s-10min (1-2min optimal), 5GB max | Explainers, event recaps, behind-the-scenes |
| **Poll** | 4 options max, 1-2 week duration | Audience research, engagement bait (use sparingly) |
| **Newsletter** | Long-form, subscriber model | Recurring thought leadership series |
| **Event** | LinkedIn Events page, can be live or external | Webinars, roundtables, conferences |
| **Live** | Requires LinkedIn Live access | Panels, Q&A, product demos |

### Format Performance Patterns (B2B)

- **Carousels/documents** consistently outperform other formats for reach in B2B. They encourage swipe-through (dwell time signal) and saves
- **Text posts** with line breaks and strong opening hooks outperform text walls
- **Native video** gets priority in feed but must hook in first 3 seconds — add captions (85% watch muted)
- **External links** in the post body reduce reach. Put links in first comment or use "link in comments" pattern
- **Polls** drive high engagement numbers but low-quality engagement — use for genuine audience research, not vanity metrics

## Posting Best Practices

### Cadence

- **Minimum**: 2-3 posts per week to maintain algorithmic visibility
- **Optimal for growth**: 4-5 posts per week (weekdays)
- **Maximum useful**: 1 post per day. Two posts in a day cannibalize each other's reach
- **Newsletter**: Weekly or biweekly. Consistency matters more than frequency

### Timing

- **B2B peak windows**: Tuesday-Thursday, 7:00-8:30 AM and 11:30 AM-1:00 PM (audience timezone)
- **Secondary window**: Tuesday-Thursday, 5:00-6:00 PM
- **Avoid**: Friday afternoons, weekends (unless audience data shows otherwise)
- **Important**: Check your own Page Analytics for actual peak times — generic guidance is a starting point, not gospel

### Engagement Protocol

- **First 60 minutes are critical**: LinkedIn's algorithm tests posts with a small audience first. Early engagement (comments especially) signals quality
- **Reply to every comment** within the first 2 hours. Substantive replies (not just "thanks!") extend reach
- **Tag people mentioned** in the post (sparingly — 2-3 max)
- **Post from admin accounts** and have leadership engage in comments within 30 minutes

## Algorithm Signals

LinkedIn's feed algorithm weighs these signals (in approximate priority order):

1. **Engagement velocity**: Comments and shares in the first hour
2. **Dwell time**: How long people spend reading/viewing before scrolling past
3. **Relevance**: Match between post topic and viewer's declared interests/engagement history
4. **Network effects**: When connections of followers engage, post enters their network's feed
5. **Content type recency**: LinkedIn periodically boosts newer format types (currently favoring carousels and newsletters)
6. **Creator consistency**: Regular posting history gets more initial distribution than sporadic posting

### What Suppresses Reach

- External links in post body (put in comments)
- Editing a post within 10 minutes of publishing
- Engagement pods (LinkedIn detects and penalizes coordinated engagement)
- Tagging 10+ people (looks like spam)
- Hashtag stuffing (3-5 relevant hashtags is optimal)
- Posting and disappearing (no replies to comments)

## Analytics

### Available Metrics (Page Admin)

- **Visitor analytics**: Page views, unique visitors, demographics (job function, seniority, industry, company size, geography)
- **Follower analytics**: Total followers, follower growth, organic vs. paid, follower demographics
- **Content analytics**: Impressions, unique impressions, clicks, reactions, comments, shares, engagement rate, video views
- **Competitor benchmarking**: Compare follower count and content engagement against up to 9 competitor pages
- **Lead gen forms**: If using lead gen CTAs, form fills and conversion rates

### Key Metrics for B2B

- **Engagement rate**: (Clicks + Reactions + Comments + Shares) / Impressions. Track by format
- **Follower growth rate**: Net new followers / total followers per period
- **Click-through rate**: Clicks / Impressions (for posts driving to website)
- **Share of voice**: Your engagement vs. competitor pages (use competitor benchmarking)

## B2B Content Strategy

### The Two-Mode Framework

LinkedIn rewards two complementary behaviors:

**Start conversations** — Original posts from your page that establish a point of view:
- Contrarian takes on industry topics (backed by evidence)
- Frameworks and mental models from your practice
- Data and research findings (original or curated with commentary)
- Client outcomes (anonymized or with permission) showing methodology in action

**Join conversations** — Engage meaningfully in others' discussions:
- Comment on industry leaders' posts with substantive additions (not "Great post!")
- Share and add perspective to relevant industry news
- Participate in trending discussions within your sector
- Reply to comments on your own posts with depth

### Thought Leadership Content Patterns

- **The framework post**: Present a named model or framework. B2B audiences save and share these
- **The contrarian insight**: Challenge a widely-held industry assumption. Must be backed by evidence
- **The case study narrative**: "Here's what happened when..." — anonymized client stories with methodology
- **The curated perspective**: Share a third-party report/article with 3-5 sentences of original analysis
- **The behind-the-curtain**: Show how your team actually works, thinks, or solves problems
- **The question post**: Ask a genuine question your audience cares about. Drives comments

### Content Mix for B2B Services

- 40% thought leadership (frameworks, insights, perspectives)
- 25% proof points (case studies, results, client wins)
- 20% industry commentary (news, trends, analysis)
- 10% culture and team (hiring, events, behind-the-scenes)
- 5% direct promotion (service launches, offers)
