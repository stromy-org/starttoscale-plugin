# AEO / GEO for Social — Getting Cited by AI & Found in Social Search

Buyers increasingly get answers from AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity,
Copilot, Google AI Overviews) and from **search inside social platforms**, not
only from the chronological/algorithmic feed. Two new discovery surfaces follow:

- **Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** — structuring content so an AI answer
  engine will surface and **cite** it in a synthesized response.
- **Generative Engine Optimization (GEO)** — earning the trust/authority signals
  that make a generative model *choose* your content when it composes an answer.
- **Social search / social SEO** — being found via the search box *within*
  LinkedIn, YouTube, Reddit, TikTok, where users increasingly search directly.

For B2B, this is not a niche add-on: social content is among the most-cited source
material for AI answers, so an organic strategy that ignores citability is leaving
its highest-authority discovery channel on the table. See `strategy-foundations.md`
Shift 2 for the evidence base.

## Which engines cite which platforms

Directional (2026), and it shifts — re-verify at review:

- **ChatGPT** — frequently cites **Reddit** (and Wikipedia).
- **Perplexity** — emphasizes **Reddit, LinkedIn, G2**.
- **Microsoft Copilot** — leans on **LinkedIn** for B2B queries.
- **Across engines** — **YouTube, Reddit, LinkedIn** are consistently among the
  most-cited domains.

**Implication:** the platforms worth optimizing for citation are, in order for
B2B, **LinkedIn, Reddit, YouTube** — which is also the strongest argument for a
Reddit presence (see `platforms/reddit.md`).

## The core principle: optimize at the passage level

Answer engines quote **sentences and short passages**, not whole pages. A single
self-contained, relevant, quotable sentence can get cited even if the rest of the
post isn't. Write so that key claims stand alone.

The three highest-leverage citation levers (per the Princeton GEO study):

1. **Named-expert quotes** (~+41% visibility) — attribute a clear claim to a real,
   named person (ties directly to `executive-led-content.md`).
2. **Statistics** (~+30%) — a specific, sourced number in a clean sentence.
3. **Explicit citations** (~+30%) — reference and link the source of a claim.

## Content patterns that get cited

- **Definitional openers** — "X is …" / "The difference between X and Y is …".
  Answer engines love clean definitions.
- **Direct-answer first** — state the answer in the first sentence, then support
  it (inverted pyramid). Don't bury the claim under a hook.
- **Q&A framing** — pose the exact question a buyer would ask an AI, then answer
  it plainly. Mirror real query language.
- **Standalone stat sentences** — "In B2B, ~84% of content sharing happens in
  private channels." One claim, one number, quotable as-is.
- **Named frameworks & numbered lists** — structured, extractable, easy for a
  model to lift as a unit.
- **Attributed POV** — "According to [named expert], …" — quotable and
  authoritative at once.

## Platform notes

- **LinkedIn** — posts, articles, and newsletters are citable and searchable.
  Put the citable claim above the "see more" fold. Fill the profile/page keyword
  fields (they feed in-platform search). Newsletters index well.
- **Reddit** — genuinely helpful, upvoted answers in the right subreddit are prime
  citation fodder (especially for ChatGPT/Perplexity). Value first, never
  spammy — Reddit punishes promotion and so, indirectly, do the engines. See
  `platforms/reddit.md`.
- **YouTube** — transcripts are indexed and cited. Say the answer out loud early,
  use descriptive titles/chapters, and write a real description with the key
  claims in text.

## What hurts citability (and reach)

- **Keyword stuffing / hashtag stuffing** — reads as spam to both algorithms and
  engines.
- **Engagement bait & vague hooks** ("You won't believe…") — no citable claim,
  and banned by the voice cascade anyway.
- **Claims with no source or number** — nothing for an engine to anchor on.
- **Walls of text with the point buried** — passage-level extraction fails.
- **Fabricated stats or quotes** — never. Unsourced claims violate the skill's
  grounding rules; a fabricated citation is worse than none.

## Measurement

Add an **AI-visibility / citation** dimension to Phase 6 (see
`measurement-benchmarks.md`):

- **Manual citation checks** — periodically ask the target buyer questions of
  ChatGPT/Perplexity/Copilot and record whether the client (or its executives) is
  named or cited, and how it's characterized.
- **Referral signal** — watch for AI-assistant referral traffic in analytics
  (small but growing; often shows as direct/unknown — pair with self-reported
  attribution).
- **Share of AI voice** — for the client's core topics, how often it appears vs
  competitors across engines.
- **In-platform search rank** — does the content surface for the searches buyers
  actually run inside LinkedIn/YouTube/Reddit?

Treat this as a slow-moving, qualitative outcome measure, not a weekly KPI.

## How this integrates

AEO/GEO is a **lens applied to content you're already making**, not a separate
content stream: when drafting pillar posts, executive POV, and long-form video,
ask "does this contain a citable, attributable, sourced claim, stated plainly?"
It composes with the voice cascade (citable ≠ robotic — clear claims can still be
in-voice) and with the executive program (named-expert quotes are the strongest
lever).
