# Community Building — B2B Reference

**Building vs managing.** `community-management.md` is *reactive* — how to respond
to comments, complaints, and crises on your own posts, with SLAs and escalation.
This file is *proactive* — how to build and participate in communities as a
distribution and trust channel. They are different disciplines; a strategy needs
both. Do not collapse "we reply to comments" into "we have a community strategy."

**Why it matters (2026).** The posture has shifted from chasing followers to
building community. An estimated **~84% of B2B sharing happens in "dark social"**
(private messages, Slack/WhatsApp/Discord, closed groups) that the public feed
never sees, and community-sourced deals reportedly close faster and at higher
value than cold outreach. See `strategy-foundations.md`, Shift 3.

## Three modes of community presence

| Mode | What | When it fits | Cost/risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Participate in others'** | Show up in existing communities (Reddit, niche Slack/Discord, industry forums, LinkedIn groups) | **Default starting point** for most B2B services firms | Low cost; must be genuinely helpful, not promotional |
| **Owned public** | Brand-hosted public group (LinkedIn/Facebook group, subreddit) | When there's a real audience and content to sustain it | Empty/dead group actively hurts credibility |
| **Owned private** | Gated Slack/Discord/circle for clients, prospects, or a niche | Mature program with a clear member value prop and a dedicated owner | High sustaining cost; dies without facilitation |

**Sequencing rule:** most clients should **participate before they build**. An
owned community is a standing commitment — an under-facilitated, half-empty
community signals decline. Earn the right to host by first being valuable in
communities that already exist.

## Participate-in-others' playbook (the default)

1. **Map the rooms.** Where does the ICP actually gather? Relevant subreddits,
   niche Slack/Discord communities, industry forums, LinkedIn/Facebook groups,
   comment sections of respected voices. (Social listening — Phase 2 — surfaces
   these.)
2. **Read the norms first.** Every community has rules and culture. Lurk, read the
   rules, understand what earns respect vs what gets removed.
3. **Value-first, promotion-almost-never.** Follow the spirit of the 90-9-1 rule:
   the vast majority of activity is helping and contributing; self-promotion is
   rare, disclosed, and only where genuinely allowed. On Reddit especially,
   promotion without contribution gets you (and your brand) burned — and, because
   answer engines cite these platforms, helpful contributions double as AEO (see
   `aeo-geo-social.md`, `platforms/reddit.md`).
4. **Use real people.** Participation comes from named humans (executives, SMEs),
   not a faceless brand account — and only real people from
   `company_context.people[]` (never fabricate a persona). This is where the
   executive/advocacy programs and community strategy meet.
5. **Bring insight back.** What the community asks and argues about is a
   free-flowing content and product-signal source. Feed it into ideation.

## Owned community (only if you can sustain it)

Before launching an owned space, confirm:

- **A clear member value proposition** — why would someone join *and stay*? (peer
  access, exclusive insight, early content, direct line to experts).
- **A named owner + facilitation rhythm** — someone whose job includes seeding
  discussion, welcoming members, and keeping it alive. Communities die from
  neglect, not from bad launches.
- **Seed content and seed members** — never open an empty room; pre-recruit a core
  of active members and a backlog of prompts.
- **A moderation posture** — reuse `community-management.md` for response norms,
  moderation rules, and escalation inside the space.

If any of these is missing, **stay in participate mode** and revisit later.

## Dark social — enable and infer what you can't see

You can't track private shares directly, but you can shape and estimate them:

- **Make content eminently shareable** — self-contained, screenshot-able,
  quotable; the point survives being forwarded without context.
- **Add low-friction share prompts** where natural (not bait) — "forward this to
  the colleague who needs it."
- **Infer, don't chase** — measure dark social via **self-reported attribution**
  ("how did you hear about us?"), spikes in direct/branded traffic after a push,
  and community-sourced conversations, not via impossible last-click tracking.
  See `measurement-benchmarks.md`.

## Measurement (community health, not vanity)

- **Participation quality** — substantive contributions made, questions answered,
  relationships formed (not post count).
- **Inbound from communities** — self-reported "found you in r/… / the X Slack".
- **Owned community (if any)** — active-member %, contribution rate, retention,
  member-sourced conversations — **not** raw member count.
- **Sentiment & narrative** — how the brand/executives are talked about in the
  rooms that matter.

## Failure modes

| Failure | Symptom | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| **Drive-by promotion** | Posts links, adds no value, gets removed/downvoted | Value-first; contribute for weeks before ever mentioning the brand |
| **Empty owned community** | Launched a group, nobody talks | Don't build until you can sustain; seed members + content; assign an owner |
| **Faceless brand presence** | A logo account lurking in human spaces | Participate as named people (execs/SMEs), never a brand shell |
| **Ignoring norms** | Removed, banned, reputation hit | Read the rules; lurk first; respect each community's culture |
| **No follow-through** | Great conversations, no capture | Feed community insight into ideation and self-reported attribution |
| **Confusing it with management** | "We reply to comments = community strategy" | Keep building (this file) and managing (`community-management.md`) distinct |
