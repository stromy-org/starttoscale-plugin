# Community Management — B2B Reference

## Response Framework by Message Type

### Praise / Positive Mention

**Goal**: Amplify and deepen the relationship.

**Response template**: Thank specifically (not generically), add value, and invite continued engagement.

- "Thank you — [specific acknowledgment of what they said]. [Related insight or question]."
- Do not just say "Thanks!" or use a heart emoji. Name what they appreciated and extend the conversation.
- If the praise is from a client, ask permission before amplifying (reposting, quoting).
- Response time: Within 4 hours during business hours.

**Example**:
> Commenter: "Your stakeholder analysis framework completely changed how we approach government engagement."
> Response: "Really glad to hear that. The framework came out of years of seeing engagement plans fail because they treated all stakeholders as a single audience. Have you adapted the segmentation approach for your specific sector?"

### Question / Information Request

**Goal**: Demonstrate expertise and drive to appropriate next step.

**Response template**: Answer directly (or acknowledge and promise follow-up), then provide a path to deeper engagement.

- Answer the question if you can do so accurately in 2-3 sentences.
- If the question requires a longer answer, reply briefly and point to a resource (blog post, video, guide).
- If the question is about services or pricing, move the conversation to DM or provide a contact path.
- Never ignore questions — unanswered questions are visible to everyone and signal disengagement.
- Response time: Within 2 hours during business hours.

**Example**:
> Commenter: "Does this approach work for government stakeholders or only private sector?"
> Response: "It works for both, but government stakeholder mapping requires an additional layer — you need to map formal authority separately from influence. We covered the government-specific adaptation in [link]. Happy to discuss further if you have a specific scenario."

### Complaint / Negative Feedback

**Goal**: De-escalate, demonstrate accountability, and move to resolution.

**Response protocol**:
1. Acknowledge the concern (do not dismiss or deflect)
2. Apologize if appropriate (be specific about what you are sorry for)
3. Move to private channel for resolution ("I'd like to understand more — could you DM us or email [address]?")
4. Do not delete the complaint unless it violates moderation rules
5. Follow up publicly after resolution ("We've connected with [name] and resolved this — thanks for bringing it to our attention")

- Response time: Within 1 hour during business hours. Complaints left unaddressed for >4 hours escalate in visibility and sentiment.
- Never argue publicly. Even if the complaint is unfair, your response is being read by hundreds of silent observers.

**Example**:
> Commenter: "We hired you for a campaign and the results were disappointing. Would not recommend."
> Response: "I'm sorry to hear your experience didn't meet expectations. That's not the standard we set for ourselves. I'd like to understand what happened — could you send us a DM or email [name]@[company].com so we can look into this properly?"

### Crisis Mention / Sensitive Topic

**Goal**: Contain, escalate internally, respond carefully.

**Response protocol**:
1. Do NOT respond immediately. Flag to the crisis lead within 15 minutes
2. Assess severity (see Crisis Protocol below)
3. Prepare a response with legal/leadership input if needed
4. Respond within 2 hours with a holding statement if full response is not ready
5. Document the interaction in the crisis log

- Response time: Internal escalation within 15 minutes. Public response within 2 hours.
- Holding statement template: "We're aware of this and are looking into it carefully. We'll share more information as soon as we can."

### Spam / Irrelevant Content

**Goal**: Remove noise without engaging.

**Action**:
- Hide or delete (platform-dependent)
- Block repeat offenders
- Do not reply to spam — engagement trains the algorithm to show more
- Report to platform if it violates terms of service

### Tone Guidelines

- **Use first person**: "I" for individual responses, "we" for company positions
- **Be conversational**: Write like you are talking to a smart colleague, not drafting a press release
- **Show personality**: It is acceptable to be slightly witty, to use analogies, to express genuine enthusiasm
- **Avoid corporate jargon**: "Leverage synergies" belongs nowhere, especially not in social media replies
- **Match energy appropriately**: If someone is excited, match their enthusiasm. If someone is frustrated, be calm and empathetic
- **Never be sarcastic**: It reads poorly in text and can be screenshot-weaponized
- **Use the person's name**: If their profile shows a real name, use it. "Great question, Sarah" is warmer than "Great question"

### Platform Tone Adjustments

| Platform | Tone Adjustment |
|----------|----------------|

| Instagram | Warmer, more visual language, emojis acceptable (sparingly) |
| Facebook | Conversational, community-oriented |
| X | Direct, concise, sharper opinions acceptable |
| YouTube | Encouraging, educational, conversational |

## Response Time Expectations

### SLA Tiers

| Tier | Message Type | Target Response Time | Maximum Response Time |
|------|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **P1 — Critical** | Crisis mention, legal risk, client complaint | 15 min (internal escalation) | 2 hours (public response) |
| **P2 — High** | Direct questions, negative feedback, service inquiries | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| **P3 — Standard** | Positive mentions, general comments, shares | 4 hours | 24 hours |
| **P4 — Low** | Reactions only (likes, emoji), generic tags | Best effort | 48 hours |

### Business Hours Definition

- Define business hours explicitly (e.g., Monday-Friday 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM AEST)
- SLA timers only run during business hours
- After-hours monitoring covers P1 only (see After-Hours section)
- Weekends: P1 monitoring only unless a campaign or event is active

## Escalation Matrix

### Design Principles

- Every message type has a clear owner and escalation path
- Escalation is not failure — it is the system working correctly
- Document escalation triggers explicitly so community managers do not hesitate

### Template Escalation Matrix

| Trigger | First Responder | Escalation To | Timeframe |
|---------|----------------|---------------|-----------|
| Standard comment/question | Community manager | — | Respond within SLA |
| Negative client feedback | Community manager | Account director | Within 30 min |
| Service complaint (public) | Community manager | Client services lead | Within 30 min |
| Legal or regulatory mention | Community manager | Legal/compliance | Within 15 min |
| Crisis mention | Community manager | Crisis lead + MD | Within 15 min |
| Media inquiry via social | Community manager | Comms/PR lead | Within 15 min |
| Executive or VIP engagement | Community manager | Relevant executive | Within 1 hour |
| Competitor attack or comparison | Community manager | Strategy/comms lead | Within 1 hour |
| Data breach or security mention | Community manager | IT security + legal | Immediately |

## Crisis Protocol

### Phase 1: Detection (0-15 minutes)

**Signals to monitor**:
- Sudden spike in mentions or tags
- Negative sentiment clustering
- Mentions by journalists or media accounts
- Client or partner tagging the company in complaints
- Internal alert that something has gone wrong

**Immediate actions**:
1. Screenshot everything (posts can be deleted by others)
2. Assess severity using the matrix below
3. Notify crisis lead via direct message (not email — speed matters)
4. Do NOT post anything public yet
5. Pause any scheduled content

### Phase 2: Assessment (15-30 minutes)

| Severity | Description | Response Level |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Low** | Isolated complaint, no media attention, contained | Community manager handles with standard response |
| **Medium** | Multiple complaints, minor media mention, limited spread | Comms lead drafts response, MD approves |
| **High** | Widespread attention, major media coverage, legal risk | Crisis team activates, external counsel if needed |

### Phase 3: Response (30 min - 2 hours)

- Draft response with appropriate stakeholders
- Post a holding statement if full response is not ready
- Respond on the platform where the crisis originated first, then cross-post
- Assign one spokesperson account for consistency
- Continue monitoring for escalation

### Phase 4: Escalation Management (2-24 hours)

- Update response as new information becomes available
- Brief internal stakeholders (staff, leadership, board if needed)
- Prepare FAQs for client-facing staff
- Monitor sentiment trajectory (improving, stable, worsening)

### Phase 5: Post-Mortem (Within 1 week)

- Document timeline of events and responses
- Identify what triggered the crisis and whether it was foreseeable
- Assess response effectiveness (speed, tone, outcome)
- Update crisis protocol based on lessons learned
- Archive all screenshots and communications

## Moderation Rules

### Hide (Not Delete)

- Off-topic comments that are not malicious
- Self-promotional comments from non-partners
- Comments in a language you cannot verify (could be spam, could be legitimate)

### Delete

- Spam (promotional links, bot-generated content)
- Profanity or personal attacks
- Misinformation presented as fact about your company
- Duplicate comments from the same user

### Escalate (Do Not Delete)

- Threats of any kind (legal, physical, reputational)
- Allegations of illegal activity
- Comments mentioning regulatory bodies or legal action
- Coordinated negative campaigns (multiple accounts, similar messaging)

### Never Delete

- Legitimate criticism or negative feedback (respond instead)
- Questions that are uncomfortable but fair
- Comments from clients, even if negative

## After-Hours Monitoring

### Minimum Viable Approach

For teams without 24/7 coverage:

1. **Set up alerts**: Platform notification for mentions, DMs, and comments on recent posts (push notifications to the on-call phone)
2. **Define on-call rotation**: One team member monitors P1 signals after hours (check every 2-3 hours or rely on push notifications)
3. **Pre-approved holding responses**: Give on-call the authority to post: "Thanks for reaching out — we'll follow up first thing tomorrow morning" for non-crisis situations
4. **Crisis-only escalation**: On-call contacts the crisis lead only for P1 severity. Everything else waits until business hours
5. **Weekend coverage**: Check once Saturday morning, once Sunday evening. Active monitoring only during campaigns or events

### What On-Call Should Do

- Acknowledge P2+ messages with a brief holding response
- Escalate P1 immediately regardless of time
- Screenshot anything concerning
- Log activity for the weekday team

### What On-Call Should NOT Do

- Engage in extended conversations
- Attempt to resolve complaints without the full team
- Post original content
- Make any promises or commitments
