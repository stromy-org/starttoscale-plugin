# Strategy Foundations — Modern B2B Organic Social

> **Purpose.** This is the evergreen strategic basis for the skill — *why* the
> workflow looks the way it does — and the research record behind the 2026
> refresh. The rest of the skill operationalizes these five shifts. When a phase
> asks "what does industry-leading practice do here?", the answer traces back to
> this file.
>
> **Last reviewed: 2026-07. Revisit annually** — platform economics and the AI
> discovery layer move fast; treat specific percentages as directional (they age
> within ~12 months) and the *shifts* as durable. Re-baseline the numbers and the
> platform list at each review. Sources consulted are listed at the end.

The through-line: B2B organic has moved from a **brand-page-centric,
plan-and-publish** discipline to a **person-led, AI-discovered,
community-anchored** one. Reach is no longer bought with a company logo and a
posting calendar; it is earned through credible individuals, citable substance,
and genuine communities. Everything below follows from that.

---

## Shift 1 — Distribution is person-led, not page-led

The single largest change. Company-page organic reach has collapsed while
personal profiles now dominate feed allocation.

- Company-page organic reach on LinkedIn has cratered to roughly **1.6% of
  followers**; personal profiles take an estimated **~65% of feed allocation vs
  ~5% for company pages**.
- Employee/individual posts generate on the order of **14× the engagement** of
  brand-page content and materially more reach.
- **Executive/founder-led content** is now the highest-leverage play and is
  *distinct* from employee advocacy: reported **~73% lower cost per qualified
  engagement and ~4× conversion** vs company-sponsored content, and C-suite
  participation drives ~2.4× higher employee participation.

**Implication for the skill.** The brand Page is a **credibility and proof
anchor**, not the primary distribution engine. Strategy is person-first: an
executive/founder thought-leadership program plus employee creators carry
distribution; the Page carries legitimacy, discoverability, and the paid handoff.
See `executive-led-content.md` (the exec engine) and `employee-advocacy.md`
(the creator base) — two complementary programs, not one optional add-on.

## Shift 2 — Content must be *citable by AI*, not just rankable in feed (AEO/GEO)

Buyers increasingly get answers from AI assistants and in-platform search, not
only from the chronological/algorithmic feed. Being **cited** by an answer engine
is now a discovery channel in its own right.

- AI answer engines lean heavily on social sources: **ChatGPT frequently cites
  Reddit**, **Perplexity cites Reddit/LinkedIn/G2**, and **Microsoft Copilot
  leans on LinkedIn for B2B queries**. YouTube, Reddit, and LinkedIn are among
  the most-cited domains across engines.
- Citation-optimization is **passage-level**, not page-level: the relevance of a
  single sentence to the query drives whether a model quotes it.
- The Princeton GEO study found **expert quotes lift AI visibility ~41%**,
  **statistics ~30%**, and **explicit citations ~30%**.

**Implication for the skill.** Answer Engine Optimization / Generative Engine
Optimization (AEO/GEO) becomes a strategy-layer concept: structure posts for
citation (clear claims, embedded statistics, named-expert quotes, Q&A/definition
framing), prioritize the platforms answer engines actually cite (Reddit,
LinkedIn, YouTube), and measure citation/visibility as an outcome. See
`aeo-geo-social.md`.

## Shift 3 — Community-*building*, not just community-*management*

The defining posture is "community over follower count." Most B2B sharing — and a
growing share of buying conversations — happens in spaces the feed never sees.

- An estimated **~84% of B2B content sharing happens in "dark social"**: Slack
  DMs, WhatsApp groups, private LinkedIn messages, email forwards, and closed
  communities.
- Community-sourced deals reportedly close faster, at higher value, and with
  stronger win rates than cold outreach (reply rates now often <6%); firms with
  strong communities are cited as growing revenue materially faster.

**Implication for the skill.** Separate two disciplines the old skill conflated.
Reactive **community management** (SLAs, moderation, crisis — `community-management.md`)
stays, but a proactive **community-building** layer joins it: owned/private
spaces, a presence in third-party communities (Reddit, niche Slack/Discord,
industry forums), and participating in *others'* conversations as a first-class
activity. See `community-building.md`.

## Shift 4 — Video-first, with a long-form comeback; creator-style beats corporate polish

- Short-form vertical video is now the **default** content format, not a trend;
  video consistently out-reaches static formats.
- **Long-form is quietly returning** — creator-driven long-form is reported to
  generate ~10× the views and ~3× the save rate of short clips, and a majority of
  buyers say long-form makes a brand feel more credible.
- **Creator/UGC-style content out-performs polished brand talk** — audiences
  trust faces, opinions, and real stakes over scripted corporate posts. This
  extends to **external B2B creator/industry-voice partnerships**, now a
  legitimate organic play.

**Implication for the skill.** Bias the format mix toward video (short-form for
discovery, long-form for depth/credibility), frame content around real people and
recurring "characters," and treat external-creator collaboration as an option in
the creative system. Carousels remain strong for saves/dwell but are no longer
the unquestioned reach king.

## Shift 5 — Measure the dark funnel, not the last click

Attribution built on UTMs and last-click systematically undercounts organic
social, because most influence now happens off-platform and unlinked.

- Standard attribution is estimated to **miss 70%+ of the B2B buying journey**,
  leaving a large share of pipeline unattributable.
- Leading teams are shifting to **self-reported attribution** ("how did you hear
  about us?") as a *primary* signal, plus marketing-mix modeling, brand/narrative
  lift, and share-of-voice — supplementing (not replacing) UTMs.

**Implication for the skill.** Elevate self-reported attribution to a primary
KPI, add dark-funnel framing, brand/narrative-lift and share-of-voice measures,
and an **AI-citation share** dimension (from Shift 2). Person-level attribution
(which executive/creator drove the signal) matters more than page-level. See
`measurement-benchmarks.md`.

## A note on using the numbers

Every percentage above is **directional and sourced to 2026 industry reporting**,
not a guarantee. Use them to justify *direction* (person-led, citable,
community-anchored, video-first, dark-funnel measurement), never as targets to
hit. Set client targets from the client's own baseline (see
`measurement-benchmarks.md`). Re-verify the figures at the next annual review.
