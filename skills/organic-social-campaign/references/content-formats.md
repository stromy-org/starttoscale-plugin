# Content Format Taxonomy — B2B Organic Social

> **Video-first (2026).** Bias the mix toward video: short-form vertical for discovery, long-form for depth and credibility. Video out-reaches static formats, and long-form is resurging as scroll fatigue grows (creator-driven long-form reportedly earns ~10× the views and ~3× the save rate of short clips, and a majority of buyers say it makes a brand feel more credible). Carousels remain strong for saves and dwell but are no longer the unquestioned reach king. Creator/UGC-style framing — real faces, real stakes — beats polished corporate talk, including **external B2B creator / industry-voice partnerships**, now a legitimate organic play. See `strategy-foundations.md`, Shift 4.

## Format Definitions

### Text Post

**Description**: Plain text, no media attachment. Relies entirely on copy quality.

**B2B use cases**: Hot takes on industry news, quick frameworks, questions to spark discussion, contrarian perspectives, personal anecdotes with professional lessons.

**Production requirements**: None beyond writing. Fastest format to produce.

**Engagement characteristics**: High comment rate when the text is provocative or asks a genuine question. Low save rate. Short lifespan (hours on X, 1-2 days on LinkedIn). Works best from personal accounts, not brand pages.

**Tips**: Use line breaks aggressively. Front-load the hook. On LinkedIn, the "see more" fold is at ~210 characters — everything above that line determines whether anyone reads the rest.

---

### Single Image

**Description**: One static image (photo, graphic, data visualization, quote card) with caption.

**B2B use cases**: Data charts from reports, branded quote cards, event photos, infographics, team photos, process diagrams.

**Production requirements**: Graphic design tool (Canva, Figma, or equivalent). Maintain brand-consistent templates. Recommended sizes vary by platform — design for the primary platform, crop for others.

**Engagement characteristics**: Moderate reach. Stops the scroll better than text alone. Data visualizations and charts drive saves. Quote cards drive shares. Team photos drive reactions.

**Tips**: One idea per image. If you need to convey multiple points, use a carousel instead. Alt text improves accessibility and provides additional context to platform algorithms.

---

### Carousel / Document

**Description**: Multi-page swipeable content. Uploaded as PDF on LinkedIn, multi-image on Instagram, not natively supported on X.

**B2B use cases**: Frameworks and models, step-by-step guides, checklists, case study summaries, report highlights, training content, "X things I learned from Y" listicles.

**Production requirements**: Design tool for consistent slide templates. 1080x1350px (portrait) recommended for LinkedIn and Instagram. Keep text large — many viewers are on mobile. 8-12 slides is the sweet spot. Include a title slide and a CTA slide.

**Engagement characteristics**: Strong organic reach on LinkedIn, with high dwell time (algorithm signal), high save rate, and moderate share rate — though video now leads for discovery. Lower comment rate than text posts.

**Tips**: Design each slide to be understandable independently (people screenshot individual slides). Use a numbered framework or progression to encourage swiping through. The title slide is your hook — treat it like a headline.

---

### Short-Form Video (Under 90 Seconds)

**Description**: Vertical video optimized for mobile-first feeds. Reels (Instagram/Facebook), Shorts (YouTube), or video posts on LinkedIn/X.

**B2B use cases**: Quick tips, behind-the-scenes moments, event highlights, "one thing I wish I knew about X", response to trending topics, humanizing the brand.

**Production requirements**: Smartphone with decent camera, basic editing app. Captions are mandatory (80%+ watch muted). Vertical format (9:16). Hook in first 2 seconds. No long intros.

**Engagement characteristics**: High reach (algorithm-favored on most platforms). Lower engagement quality (views are easy, comments are hard). Good for discovery — new audiences find you through short-form. Lower save rate than carousels.

**Tips**: One idea per video. State the takeaway in the first 3 seconds. End with a question or CTA, not a fade-out. Repurpose across platforms but re-export natively for each (avoid watermarks).

---

### Long-Form Video (Over 90 Seconds)

**Description**: Produced video content, typically horizontal (16:9). Primary home is YouTube; cross-posted to LinkedIn and Facebook.

**B2B use cases**: Deep explainers, methodology walkthroughs, case study narratives, webinar recordings, interview series, conference talks.

**Production requirements**: Camera, microphone, lighting, editing software. Minimum viable: webcam + USB mic + ring light. Thumbnails must be custom-designed. Chapters/timestamps for navigation.

**Engagement characteristics**: High watch time (valuable algorithm signal on YouTube). Builds deep credibility. Long content lifespan (months to years on YouTube). Lower volume metrics than short-form but higher quality engagement. Resurging in 2026 as audiences seek depth (see the video-first note above) — and transcripts are indexed and cited by AI answer engines (see `aeo-geo-social.md`).

**Tips**: Front-load value — do not waste the first 30 seconds on intros or logos. Structure with chapters. Edit ruthlessly — remove pauses, tangents, and filler. 8-15 minutes is the B2B sweet spot.

---

### Poll

**Description**: Multiple-choice question with timed voting. Available on LinkedIn, X, Facebook, YouTube (Community tab), and Instagram (Stories).

**B2B use cases**: Audience research, conversation starters, gauging sentiment on industry topics, pre-event engagement.

**Production requirements**: None.

**Engagement characteristics**: High participation rate (low friction to engage). Low-quality engagement (clicking a button requires no thought). Can feel gimmicky if overused. Most valuable when the results inform future content.

**Tips**: Use sparingly (once per 2 weeks maximum). Ask genuine questions where you want the data, not engagement-bait questions. Share results and your analysis as a follow-up post. 2-3 options perform better than 4.

---

### Newsletter

**Description**: Recurring long-form publication delivered to subscribers. LinkedIn Newsletters and Substack/Beehiiv (cross-posted to social).

**B2B use cases**: Recurring thought leadership series, industry analysis, curated insights, research summaries.

**Production requirements**: Consistent writing schedule (weekly or biweekly). 800-1,500 words per issue. Cover image per issue.

**Engagement characteristics**: Subscribers get notified — guaranteed distribution to your audience. Builds a direct relationship. LinkedIn Newsletters notify all followers on first issue. High commitment format — readers who subscribe are engaged.

**Tips**: Establish a clear value proposition (what will subscribers learn and how often). Use a consistent structure readers can rely on. Cross-promote newsletter content as standalone posts.

---

### Event

**Description**: Hosted event pages (LinkedIn Events, Facebook Events) for webinars, conferences, meetups.

**B2B use cases**: Webinar promotion, conference attendance, roundtable discussions, product launches, community meetups.

**Production requirements**: Event logistics (date, time, description, speakers, registration link). Promotional content plan (3-4 posts leading up to the event).

**Engagement characteristics**: RSVPs create a committed audience. LinkedIn Events notify attendees' networks. Facebook Events still drive registrations for local and regional events. Post-event content (recordings, key takeaways) extends the value.

**Tips**: Create the event 3-4 weeks in advance. Post speaker spotlights and topic teasers in the lead-up. Go live during the event for additional reach. Post a recap with key insights after.

---

### Live

**Description**: Real-time video broadcast. Available on LinkedIn (requires access), YouTube, Facebook, Instagram.

**B2B use cases**: Panels, Q&A sessions, product demonstrations, conference simulcasts, breaking news commentary.

**Production requirements**: Stable internet connection, camera/webcam, microphone. LinkedIn Live requires application approval. Promotion plan (audiences do not stumble upon B2B live streams).

**Engagement characteristics**: Real-time comments create urgency and engagement. Low replay value unless edited into clips. Requires pre-promotion to build an audience. Works best with 2+ speakers (solo live streams struggle to sustain attention).

**Tips**: Promote 1-2 weeks in advance. Have a moderator manage comments. Prepare an outline but do not script. Record and repurpose into clips, quotes, and a summary post. Test all tech 30 minutes before.

## Format x Funnel Stage Matrix

| Format | Awareness | Consideration | Conversion |
|--------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Text post | Medium | Low | Low |
| Single image | High | Low | Low |
| Carousel/Document | High | High | Medium |
| Short-form video | High | Medium | Low |
| Long-form video | Medium | High | Medium |
| Poll | Medium | Low | Low |
| Newsletter | Low | High | High |
| Event | Medium | High | High |
| Live | Medium | Medium | Medium |

**Reading the matrix**:
- **Awareness**: Reaches new audiences, builds brand recognition
- **Consideration**: Educates, builds trust, demonstrates expertise
- **Conversion**: Drives action (website visit, download, inquiry, registration)

Carousels and long-form video are the most versatile formats for B2B because they work across multiple funnel stages.

## Hero → derivative production (COPE)

"Create once, publish everywhere" is only affordable if it is **planned**, not
improvised per post. A campaign needing 36–48 posts across two tracks cannot
produce 40 original assets — it produces a handful of **hero** assets and fans
each into **derivatives**. Planned this way the posts also stay *coherent*,
because they inherit their argument from a shared source.

### The flow

The most efficient production workflow creates once and adapts:

1. **Start with long-form** (YouTube video or LinkedIn carousel/article)
2. **Extract short-form** (Reels/Shorts from key moments, or individual carousel slides as single images)
3. **Write text versions** (Thread on X, text post on LinkedIn summarizing key points)
4. **Create engagement hooks** (Poll asking about the topic, question post inviting discussion)

This gives you 4-6 pieces of content from one core idea, each native to its platform.

### Hero assets

A **hero** is the flagship an act hangs off — a research explainer, factsheet,
mini-documentary, or animated data story. In campaign mode, plan **1–2 heroes
per act** (see [campaign-narrative-arc.md](campaign-narrative-arc.md)); in
program mode, plan them per pillar only when the user asks.

### Derivative map

Each hero fans into **3–6 satellites per track**:

- carousel slides → single images
- video → clips + quote cards
- document → a text-post series
- data → a chart, then an animated reveal

**Derivative-count guidance: a hero yields 4–6 derivatives before fatigue.** Past
that the audience recognises the source and the returns invert. When an act needs
more volume, **refresh the hero — don't stretch it**.

### Producer mapping (informational)

Which capability typically renders each derivative. This is **orientation, not
instruction** — this skill never invokes another skill; the user runs the
producer, or Phase 8 handles the media-gen subset.

| Derivative | Platform surface | Typical producer |
|---|---|---|
| PDF carousel (document post) | LinkedIn document | HD PDF rendering (e.g. `format-pdf-hd`) |
| Data visual / comparison graphic | LinkedIn + Instagram image | chart/diagram skills |
| Animated data reveal (MP4) | both feeds | motion baking (e.g. `format-motion`) |
| Reel / short + branded imagery | Instagram reel, LinkedIn video | `media-gen` MCP (Phase 8 loop) |
| Quote card | both | media-gen or diagram |
| Text-post series | LinkedIn text | agent copy via voice cascade |

### Every derivative must be native

A derivative is **re-cut for its surface, never cross-posted verbatim**. Beyond
reading as lazy, recycled/watermarked assets are algorithmically down-ranked (see
[platforms/meta.md](platforms/meta.md)) — so the verbatim shortcut costs reach.
A carousel exported as a PDF and dropped on Instagram is not a derivative; it is
the same asset in the wrong place.
