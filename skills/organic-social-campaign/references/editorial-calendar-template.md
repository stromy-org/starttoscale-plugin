# Editorial Calendar Template — B2B Reference

## Calendar Structure

### Required Columns

| Column | Description | Example Values |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Week** | Calendar week number | W14, W15, W16, W17 |
| **Date** | Publish date | 2026-04-07 |
| **Day** | Day of week | Tuesday |
| **Platform** | Target platform | LinkedIn, Instagram, X |
| **Pillar** | Content pillar this maps to | Thought Leadership, Proof Points |
| **Series** | Recurring series name (if applicable) | "Tuesday Insights", "Client Spotlight" |
| **Concept** | Working title or brief description | "Why most stakeholder maps fail" |
| **Format** | Content format | Carousel, Text post, Video, Poll |
| **CTA** | Call to action | Comment, Visit link, Register, DM |
| **Owner** | Person responsible for creation | JS, MK, Brand team |
| **UTM Tags** | Tracking parameters | utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=q2-thought-leadership |
| **Paid Amplification** | Whether this post should be boosted | Yes/No + budget if yes |
| **Status** | Production status | Idea, Drafting, Review, Approved, Scheduled, Published |

### Optional Columns

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Messaging Pillar** | Links to messaging framework pillar |
| **Visual Brief** | Description of required visual asset |
| **Copy Draft** | Link to draft copy document |
| **Approval** | Who needs to sign off |
| **Employee Advocacy** | Should this be shared to advocate queue |
| **Repurpose From** | If adapted from another piece (blog, webinar, etc.) |
| **Performance Notes** | Post-publish engagement notes |

## Balancing Pillars Across Weeks

### Distribution Rules

- Each content pillar should appear at least once per week
- No pillar should exceed 50% of posts in any 2-week window
- Each week has a "focus" pillar (gets the flagship piece — usually the Tuesday carousel) but others still appear
- Direct promotion (service announcements, hiring) should not exceed 1 post per 2 weeks

### Pillar Rotation Pattern

A simple rotation ensures balance without being mechanical:

| Week | Focus Pillar | Tuesday Flagship | Other Posts |
|------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Industry Insights | Insights carousel | 1 Methodology, 1 Proof Point, 1 flexible |
| 2 | Methodology | Framework carousel | 1 Insights, 1 Proof Point, 1 flexible |
| 3 | Proof Points | Case study carousel | 1 Insights, 1 Methodology, 1 flexible |
| 4 | Industry Insights | Trend analysis carousel | 1 Methodology, 1 Proof Point, 1 flexible |

The "flexible" slot is for reactive content, culture posts, or employee advocacy amplification.

### Seasonal Adjustments

- **Conference season**: Increase Industry Insights posts (live coverage, speaker content, networking)
- **Budget planning season** (Oct-Dec for many B2B): Increase Proof Points (case studies, ROI data)
- **Summer / Holiday periods**: Reduce frequency slightly, lean into evergreen content and lighter formats
- **Campaign launches**: Temporarily weight toward the relevant pillar, but never go 100% on one topic

## Handling Reactive Content

### The 80/20 Rule

Plan 80% of content in advance via the calendar. Reserve 20% capacity for reactive content:

- Breaking industry news that demands commentary
- Trending topics relevant to your sector
- Timely responses to competitor announcements
- Unexpected events or opportunities

### Reactive Content Protocol

1. **Identify**: Monitor industry news feeds, trending hashtags, and competitor activity
2. **Assess**: Is this relevant to our audience? Can we add unique perspective? Is it time-sensitive?
3. **Decide**: Within 1 hour — publish reactive content or skip it
4. **Displace**: Reactive content replaces the lowest-priority scheduled post for that day (usually the "flexible" slot)
5. **Reschedule**: Displaced content moves to the next available slot or drops if no longer timely

### What Qualifies as Reactive

- Major policy or regulatory changes affecting your sector
- Significant industry report releases
- Trending LinkedIn or X conversations in your domain
- Client wins or milestones that can be shared immediately
- Industry events or breaking news

### What Does NOT Qualify

- Generic trending topics unrelated to your expertise
- Competitor drama (stay above it)
- Viral memes (unless you can adapt them authentically and quickly)
- "National [thing] Day" posts (unless genuinely relevant)

## Calendar Review Cadence

### Weekly Review (30 minutes, every Friday)

- Review performance of the past week's content (engagement, reach, clicks)
- Finalize next week's content (all posts should be in "Approved" or "Scheduled" status)
- Identify any reactive content opportunities for the coming week
- Check pillar balance — are we over-indexing on one topic?

### Monthly Review (60 minutes, last week of month)

- Aggregate performance metrics by pillar, format, and platform
- Identify top 3 and bottom 3 performing posts — analyze why
- Draft next month's calendar (concepts and formats, not full copy)
- Review employee advocacy queue performance
- Adjust pillar weights based on what the audience is responding to

### Quarterly Review (90 minutes)

- Review against strategic KPIs (follower growth, engagement trend, website traffic from social)
- Assess content pillar relevance — do they still align with business priorities?
- Review audience demographics — is the right audience engaging?
- Plan any series launches, retirements, or format experiments for next quarter
- Update benchmark targets based on actual performance data

## UTM Tagging Convention

Consistent UTM parameters enable accurate attribution. Use this structure for all social links:

```
utm_source=<platform>          # linkedin, instagram, facebook, twitter, youtube
utm_medium=organic             # always "organic" for non-paid
utm_campaign=<quarter>-<pillar> # q2-thought-leadership, q2-proof-points
utm_content=<post-identifier>  # 2026-04-08-stakeholder-map-carousel
```

**Example**: `https://company.com/insights?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=q2-methodology&utm_content=2026-04-08-stakeholder-map`

Tag every link, every time. Untraceable traffic from social is invisible in analytics and makes organic social look less effective than it actually is.

## Paid Amplification Triggers

Not every post needs boosting. Flag posts for paid amplification when:

- **High early engagement**: A post performs 2x above your average engagement rate in the first 2 hours — amplify to ride the momentum
- **Strategic priority**: The post supports a current business priority (event registration, service launch, hiring)
- **Evergreen value**: The content will remain relevant for weeks — worth putting budget behind
- **Lead generation CTA**: Posts with a direct CTA (download, register, contact) benefit most from amplification

Budget allocation per post: $25-$100 for organic amplification. Set a monthly cap (e.g., $200-$500) and allocate based on performance signals, not pre-scheduled.
